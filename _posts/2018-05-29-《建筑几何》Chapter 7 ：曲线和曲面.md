### Curves 曲线
-  All points of a circle or a conic section lie in a plane. These curves are called **planar curves**, in contrast to **spatial curves** such as helixes.

<img src="/images/posts/AG/karl.png" height="210" width="390">  

- Points of the curve with a uniquely defined tangent are called **regular points**. All points where the tangent vector $c'(t)$ is a non-vanishing vector are **regular points**. Curve points where $c'(t)$ is the zero vector $o$ are singular points.

<img src="/images/posts/AG/singular.png" height="150" width="400">  

- discrete curve 离散曲线：The polygon is also called a **discrete curve**. In many practical computations, curves are actually **polygons (i.e., discrete curves)**.

<img src="/images/posts/AG/frenet.png" height="240" width="400">

### Conic sections 锥部分
- implicit representation隐式表示：$a·x^2 + b·x·y + c·y^2 + d·x + e·y + f = 0.$
- ellipse 椭圆： $b2·x2 + a2·y2 – a2·b2 = 0$;

<img src="/images/posts/AG/ellipse.png" height="150" width="200">  

<img src="/images/posts/AG/ellipse2.png" height="300" width="400">  

- hyperbola 双曲线：$b^2·x^2 – a^2·y^2 – a^2·b^2 = 0$;

<img src="/images/posts/AG/hyperbola.png" height="170" width="320">  

<img src="/images/posts/AG/hyperbola2.png" height="230" width="430">
